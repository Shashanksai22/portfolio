Intelligent Reflecting Surface (IRS)


In my Intelligent Reflecting Surfaces (IRS) project, I employed a model featuring two IRS elements in a Single-Input Single-Output (SISO) configuration. This setup leverages the unique capability of IRS to manipulate radio frequency signals by reflecting them. Each IRS element acts as an intelligent surface that dynamically adjusts its reflective properties based on the incident signal. The SISO configuration simplifies the system while still demonstrating the fundamental impact of IRS on wireless communication.

In practical terms, this model allows for a comprehensive exploration of the influence of IRS on signal strength, phase, and overall channel characteristics. By precisely controlling the reflections from each IRS element, I aimed to optimize signal propagation, mitigate path loss, and enhance overall communication performance. This configuration serves as a foundational exploration into the practical implications of IRS in a controlled environment, providing valuable insights into the potential improvements it can bring to wireless communication systems.
